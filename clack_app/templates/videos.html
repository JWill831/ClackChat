Here are some great videos you can check out to get more information about mechanical keyboards. Some of these will be instructional, and some are just fun to check out. The comments for the video will alwawys be below the video it relates to. If you have a favorite video, contact me and I can add it here :) Happy clacking!

^Above is a great how to on building a keyboard. While different boards invlove different parts or build processes, this covers the basics for a starter kit. The board in this video is known as a 60% as it is smaller than a standard 100% keyboard.

^This video is a "sound test" video which are becoming more and more popular. A big part of the mechanical keyboard hobbie his how a board sounds! Keyboard: Neuron (aluminum) PCB: Neuron Plate: brass Switches: Gateron Inks (lubed with Krytox 104 on springs and housings, 106 on springs, TX switch films added) Keycaps: JTK Arctic Artisan: JTK Arctic Orochi from Keyforge Foam added between PCB and plate for sound dampening

^While many keyboards come equipped with software to change layouts or specific key inputs, many "custom" keyboards come "blank" and need to have firmware installed. QMK (quantum mechanical keyboard) is a community based effort to make this process more accessible to beginners. The video above shows how to use the QMK configurator to install firmware onto a keyboard.

^Like many other hobbies, part of the fun with building a mechanical keyboard is building a skill set. Many keyboard kits come disassembled and require you to actualy solder the components yourself. Whiel this may not sound fun to some, it can be enjoyable with some practice and know how! The above video demonstrates how to solder components properly, and explains what tools would be neccessary.

^Mechanical keyboards get their name from their switches being mechanical, and they can come in a wide variety of "styles." Some are make a loud click, orthers are silent, and some feel like a bump when you press them. Here is a video showing how the sound and feel might vary from one type of switch to the next.

^Keycaps are a huge part of they MK experience, and the video above demonstrates how you could make your own "artisan" keycaps. There are many many tutorials online, and as many vendors out there to purchase them from.
