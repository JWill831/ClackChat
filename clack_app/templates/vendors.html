Here are a few great vendors to check out for upcoming projects. If would like to see a vendor added to this seciton, feel free to reach out! Happy clacking!

^NovelKeys Here you can find some great products from an ever growing vendor. This site has really grown in the last few years, and is tuned in to current market trends. Most notibly, this site has a huge selection of switches.

^KBDfans One of the most recognizable names in the market, KBDfans is where a lot of people start in the hobby. They have great customer service, and offer high quality products at relatively inexpensive prices. They offer many 'kits' that are great for newcomers.

^SpacecatDesigns This site is a great place to find fun projects to build your skill set. It features many DIY options that do take some research and know how to put together. If you are looking for something more than a generic kit, check them out.

^WASD Keyboards This company provides inexpensive custom keycaps sets, and a decent selection of premade keyboards. They let you upload basic images on keycaps for a very custom look. Their customer service is very helpful, and they have very fast shipping times.

^Keeb.io This site is another great DIY project hub. With lots of parts, kits, and accessories, you can really get deep into a custom set up. Some of the projects requeire a deeper understanding of how to program firmware, but there are also resources on the site to help with that.

^Drop Formerly known as MassDrop, this site offers a wide variety of keyboard products. This site has somehow created a bit of a love or hate situation, but overall they provide good customer service. This is not a bad place to start while just getting into the hobbie.

^Mechanicalkeyboards This site is a great place for very standardized and plug 'n play set ups. They offer a decent variety of accessories for the handful of boards that they offer.

^Geekhack Here you can find a number of community based vendors that offer limited relase productions. While it is not always up to date, it's fun to see what comes out as they tend to be fairly exclusive. boards that they offer.

^/r/mechmarket This subreddit is one of the best places to look for either aftermarket items, or limited groupbuy releases. While there are a lot of rules to follow on this site, it is a great resource to either sell or buy products in the community.

^PrimeKeyboards PrimeKB is a one man operation that has been bringing innovative products to the market for years. Here you can find unique keyboard products, and the customer service is outstanding.

^Keyboardbelle KBbelle site has grown tremendously in the past year. This site went from offer a few prints here and there to a full fledge ordering site with tons of options. These cases are 3dprinted for a unique look and sound. They are relatively inexpensive, and offer an interesting typing experience.

^Zap Cables A fun part of the MK hobby is diy projects, and this site is great for getting into making custom cables. They offer everything you need in a kit, or you can go ahead and custom order a premade one.

^pimpmykeyboard PMK has been a huge part of the MK community in that they offer a huge variety of keycaps. If you need just an odd cap for a special layout, they prorbably have it. Once in a while, they offer a grabbag which can be pretty fun to play with.

If you have a vendor you would like to see here, feel free to reach out!
